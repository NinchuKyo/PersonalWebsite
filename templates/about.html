About

Here's Some Fun Facts About Myself!

I enjoy a good cup of freshly ground coffee prepared using a french press in the morning, my video gaming days started with the NES, I enjoy cooking and trying out different types of food, and I'm not afraid to go out of my comfort zone in order to learn.

My favourite programming language so far would have to be Python, as I really enjoy how it is dynamic typed, the syntax (in other languages, too much braces, especially with nested-if-else statements makes lines look too cluttered and hard to read), how its easy to get up and running, and the ease at which one could add libraries using PIP (Package Manager for Python).

C# is also fairly close up there with its ease-of-use features such as IntelliSense, the utility of its libraries, and how well it works on a Windows system. I often program using Vim as my text-editor as it can be as thin or thick as I want it to be through additional plugins, but I don't mind using Visual Studio as an IDE, as it is full of useful features, especially IntelliSense (did I not mention IntelliSense...?).

Cooking

While I was in University, I worked part-time as a cook. It was interesting, but it was hard work. The best part about cooking is making up your own recipes with whatever you have in the fridge.

Originally, I wanted to be a chemist when I first got into university, and since chemistry is like cooking, it was natural that I would enjoy cooking.

As I progressed, I gradually figured out that I enjoyed creating things, experimenting, and learning more, thus computer science and software development felt perfect for me.

Always being told that you're wrong by the computer is an interesting feeling, but always gives you perspective that you can keep on learning. Same with the feeling of cooking, when a recipe doesn't turn out as expected.

But the moment when you get it right, and it all comes together, is what makes it worth it all in the end, for both software development and cooking.
